# Engineering Principles

## Complexity must justify itself

Every additional component, dependency or abstraction has a maintenance cost.

Complexity should only exist when its benefits clearly outweigh its operational cost.

---

## Optimize for long term maintainability

The best solution is rarely the cleverest one.

Good systems are understandable, predictable and easy to evolve.

---

## Invest effort where it creates the most value

Engineering is about trade offs.

Not every problem deserves the same investment.

Prioritize according to impact.

---

## Automate repetitive work

Human effort should be invested in solving new problems, not repeating old ones.

Automation reduces operational risk and frees engineers to focus on higher value work.

---

## Hide complexity

Users and operators should interact with simple interfaces.

Implementation complexity belongs inside the system, not at its boundaries.
